South New Street

Seeing the Impact of my work (7 of the 10 buildings on S. New Street block) and its resulting commercial revitalization and tourist destination.

New Street Parking lot (before)

Location: Staunton, VA
Original Date of Construction: New construction

New Street Parking Garage

Location: Staunton, VA
Original Date of Construction: New construction
Highlights: All Star Public Improvement Award, Staunton Downtown Development Association
Collaboration: State of Virginia

R.R. Smith Center for History and Art

Location: Staunton, VA
Original Date of Construction: 1896
Building Use: Non-profit offices, art gallery and archives
Highlights: Original design by noted local historic architect T.J. Collins

Union Bus Terminal

Location: Staunton, VA
Building Use : Commerical space on first floor, upper story apartments
Highlights: State and federal historic tax credits

16-18 South New Street (before)

Location: Staunton, VA
Original Date of Construction: Late 1800s
Building Use: First floor retail and upper story apartments
Highlights: Commercial Rehabilitation Award, Historic Staunton Foundation Preservation Awards: 16-18 S. New Street, Staunton, VA

16 S. New (after)

Location: Staunton, VA
Original Date of Construction: Late 1800s

Cranberry's Building

Location: Staunton, VA
Original Date of Construction: 1758 (one of the oldest standing in Staunton)
Building Use: Retail and restaurant
Highlights: According to Cranberry's website: Originally a stone structure, it now sports a brick façade added during the Victorian era to modernize its appearance. Over the years the building has served as a women’s clothing store, New York deli, tailor’s shop, taxi garage, Chinese laundry, and general mercantile.

*Completed as project manager and executive architect during my tenure with Frazier Associates, Staunton, VA
